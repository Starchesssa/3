At Amazon, frugality wasn't just about saving money; it was a mindset that truly fueled innovation. In the early days, Jeff Bezos famously made desks out of old doors, setting a humble tone for the entire company. This wasn't because they couldn't afford better; it was a deliberate choice to operate lean.

This scrappy approach meant every team had to think creatively about how to solve problems without endless resources. Instead of buying expensive software, they often built their own, leading to unique solutions tailored precisely to their needs. This forced ingenuity, constantly pushing them to invent and simplify, rather than relying on costly, off-the-shelf options. It proved that limitations, when embraced, can be the greatest catalyst for groundbreaking ideas and efficient, scalable systems that lead to massive success.
